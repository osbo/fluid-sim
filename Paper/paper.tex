% --- 1. INTRODUCTION ---
\section{Introduction}
% Introduce the demand for real-time fluid simulation and the bottleneck of solving SPD linear systems.
% Discuss the limitations of classical preconditioners (e.g., AMG memory overhead and poor GPU parallelization for setup) and existing neural methods.
% Introduce your solution: A learning-based Sparse Approximate Inverse (SPAI) preconditioner utilizing a Recursive Hierarchical Transformer.
% Clearly list your core contributions in a bulleted list (e.g., the architecture, the novel loss function, the real-time performance achievements).

Real-time fluid simulation often reduces to repeatedly solving large, sparse symmetric positive definite linear systems, for example in pressure projection steps~\citep{Lyu2026multigrid}. Classical multigrid and Krylov methods remain strong baselines~\citep{Luz2020learning}, while learning-based approaches have targeted preconditioners and fast surrogate operators~\citep{Li2023learning, Chen2025graph, Yang2025sparse, Xu2025neural}. Hierarchical and low-rank structure appears both in classical fast algorithms and in recent neural architectures for Green's operators and PDE maps~\citep{Fan2019multiscale, Fognini2025neural, Sittoni2026neural, Yang2026lowrank}. Differentiable simulation and inverse problems further motivate fast linear algebra inside optimization loops~\citep{Ma2023neural, Hartland2023hodlr, Li2024neuralfluid, Li2025adjoint}. We develop a learning-based sparse approximate inverse (SPAI) preconditioner built from a recursive hierarchical transformer (\textsc{LeafOnly} architecture) for use inside preconditioned conjugate gradient (PCG) solves.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Architecture.} Recursive hierarchical transformer preconditioner with block tokens, hierarchical attention, GCN layers, and highway connections (Section~\ref{sec:method}).
  \item \textbf{Training.} Self-supervised objective based on Hutchinson-type estimators (cosine vs.\ $L_2$ variants) for operator quality without paired ground-truth solves.
  \item \textbf{Performance.} GPU-friendly SPAI structure avoiding triangular solves; target real-time total solve time with reported iteration counts and residuals vs.\ baselines (Jacobi, IC, AMGX, unpreconditioned CG).
\end{itemize}
